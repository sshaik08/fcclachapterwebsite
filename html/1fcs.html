Promotion of Family and Consumer Sciences

What is FCS? Why is it important?

The American Association of Family and Consumer Sciences (AAFCS) defines Family and Consumer Sciences (FCS) as a “field of study focused on the science and the art of living and working well in our complex world” and individuals who study FCS focus on helping people through research, education, and technology (“What is FCS?”).

The primary content areas of FCS include culinary arts, hospitality, tourism, education, food science, nutrition, health management, housing and interior design, family relations, human development, finances, apparel, and retail. These focuses all implement aspects of sustainability, conscious consumption, and leadership (“What is FCS?”).

FCS & College and Career Readiness

Through FCS, students are empowered and prepared for success in college and career as they are taught essential life skills, such as financial literacy, nutrition, and human development.

What is FCCLA? Why is it important?

Family, Career and Community Leaders of America, or FCCLA, is a national organization for students in the FCS program.

Career Clusters

The six FCCLA Career Clusters are categories that align careers and industries with similar skills, knowledge, and goals. They help students explore career options, connect classroom education to real-world jobs, and make future plans. Career clusters broaden the range of interconnected careers a student can study.

  • Arts & Design

    Focuses on careers that use creativity and design to improve products, spaces, and experiences. Includes fashion, interior design, visual arts, and creative problem-solving. These careers focus on aesthetics, innovation, and functionality.

  • Hospitality & Tourism

    Focuses on careers related to service, travel, food, and guest experiences. Includes careers in culinary arts, lodging, tourism, and event planning. These careers emphasize customer service, organization, and experience creation.

  • Financial Services

    Focuses on careers involving money management and financial decision-making. Includes budgeting, banking, investing, and financial planning careers. These roles emphasize accuracy, analysis, and responsible planning.

  • Education & Training

    Focuses on careers that support teaching, learning, and skill development. Includes teaching, training, and educational support roles. These careers emphasize communication, leadership, and mentorship.

  • Human Services

    Focuses on careers dedicated to helping people and improving quality of life. Includes counseling, family services, wellness, and community support careers. These roles emphasize empathy, problem-solving, and care.

  • Public Service

    Focuses on careers that serve communities and promote public well-being. Includes public safety, health, and government-related roles. These careers emphasize responsibility, leadership, and civic involvement.

FCCLA & College and Career Readiness

FCCLA helps build career readiness skills through projects, leadership experiences, and competitive events. Guided by the FCCLA Career Clusters, students develop communication, teamwork, and problem-solving skills.
